## 2. What the engine gained

- **Fog of war (GAME-01).** `visible_enemies` and the new
  `map_info.hazards` are computed per player as the union of alive
  friendly bots' scan radii (Euclidean, `SCAN_FLOOR = 2` so nothing is
  blind to an adjacent enemy). Scan stats stopped being dead numbers:
  NanoExplorer became the team's eyesight.
- **Line-of-sight combat (GAME-03).** `_line_blocked()` (Bresenham over
  the segment) blocks shots crossing Bone or any alive NanoWall —
  either player's, a wall is a physical barrier. Blocked shots log an
  `attack_blocked` event. The requirement's acceptance criterion is a
  test: a needle ringed by walls takes zero damage from a lone attacker.
- **Habitas exclusivity (GAME-04).** Needle-on-occupied-cell builds fail
  with `habitas_occupied`; the point reopens when the needle dies. Ends
  the v0.0.8 silent-score-theft case.
- **White-cell hazards (GAME-02).** Map-declared patrols (looping
  waypoint path, HP, damage, contact range, speed) that move one
  Manhattan step per cooldown, are blocked by walls/Bone, slowed by
  blockers, and bite the nearest bot of either player in range.
  Collectors can shoot them (same defend() path, LOS applies). Frames
  record hazard state; death events (`bot_destroyed` with a cause,
  `hazard_destroyed`) now exist for both combat and hazard kills —
  they also power the viewer's new event ticker.

## 3. Balance work was traced, not guessed

Three iterations on `example_defense`, each driven by replay traces:

1. First rematch after LOS shipped: **zero blocked shots** — walls
   built at T34/T85 had expired ~200 turns before the first shot
   (T326). Walls are 50-turn consumables; the demo treated them as
   permanent.
2. Standing 3-wall arc + banking upkeep: still zero blocks — banking
   never started because the funding threshold (needle ≥ 30) was
   unreachable before the raid arrived; and the arithmetic is
   fundamental: a 3-wall arc costs ~1.5 AZN/turn upkeep against a
   collector economy netting ~0.25/turn. Standing fortifications are
   economically impossible *by design*.
3. The affordable pattern that shipped: **reactive defense** — an
   Explorer watchtower parked on the needle (scan 30 vs the attacker's
   range 12) plus the NanoAI garrisoning the needle and dropping a wall
   on the exact firing line only when a raider is spotted. Builds
   resolve before attacks in the turn order, so the wall beats the
   shot. Result: the siege that previously ended by T630 ran to T1056+
   with the needle intact for 1000+ turns.

Tournament calibration: after the rework `example_defense` initially
collapsed to 4W-17L because it hoarded wall money even in peaceful
matches (capping its needle at 40 pts/turn forever). A 60-AZN war-chest
cap — save that much, then resume feeding the needle — restored it to
3rd place (15W, 2810 pts). Final 84-match leaderboard tells the intended
story: pure aggression still wins head-to-heads but scores less overall;
scouting/defense/economy are all viable; the zero-score invariant from
v0.0.10 still holds (only needle-kill sieges zero anyone, ~3/21).

`example_combat` and `example_full_roster` also needed fog updates: a
fighter's own scan is ~0, so combat now pairs it with an Explorer
spotter sweeping the enemy's mirror corner (artillery + forward
observer), and full_roster's fighter guards its needle when nothing is
spotted instead of stopping in the open.

## 4. Maps (GAME-05, "change the map if required")

- **Vascular Network**: two white-cell patrols added (a horizontal sweep
  through the northern resource belt, a vertical mid-map lane), each
  waypoint verified passable before writing.
- **Bone Maze** (new, 50×50): concentric bone rings with offset gaps,
  two stream arteries as fast lanes, a center-prize habitas plus four
  pocket objectives, rim/inner AZN nodes, and two patrols. Generated by
  script; every habitas point and AZN node verified reachable from both
  spawn zones with the real pathfinder, `validate()` clean, hazards
  round-trip through save/load — all before the file was written.
- **Simple Tissue** left hazard-free deliberately: the beginner map.

## 5. Watchability & UX ("informative on the simulations")

The viewer now answers "what just happened?" without guesswork: attack
tracers (gold), blocked-shot tracers (gray), hazard bites (white), and
the fx animations (impact/build/collect/destruct) at the exact cell,
looping while a frame is current so stepping through turns is
self-explanatory. The HUD gained an **Events** ticker — the last five
notable events up to the current turn (builds, kills with cause,
expiries, new injection points) — plus a white-cell legend row. The
match window gained **Replays…** (open any saved replay, tournament
games included — closes the UX-01 gap where those were unviewable) and
**seed display with a lock toggle** (UX-04; the menu's first match is
random-seeded now too). The main menu uses the background art with the
baked-in title; buttons moved to the lower third after a first
screenshot showed them overlapping the art's title panel.

Editor ("intuitive for the creator"): a Zone Owner P1/P2 toggle in the
sidebar (closing the MAP-08 authoring gap for zones), the status bar
names the owner new zones get, and hazard patrols render as
route-polylines + white blobs so a loaded map's hazards are visible
instead of silently round-tripping. Hazard *authoring* remains
JSON-only — MAP-08 stays 🟡 with an editor hazard tool as the remaining
gap.
